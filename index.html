1.

The National Drug Law Enforcement Agency (NDLEA) was established by Decree No. 48 of 29th December, 1989 during the military administration of General Ibrahim Babangida, GCFR. The Agency was created to coordinate and enforce all laws and regulations on narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances within Nigeria. Prior to the establishment of NDLEA, drug law enforcement responsibilities were fragmented across several agencies including the Nigeria Customs Service, the Nigeria Police Force, and the National Security Organisation. The alarming proliferation of illicit drug trafficking and abuse across Nigeria, and the consequent threat to national security and public health, necessitated the creation of a dedicated lead anti-narcotics agency. NDLEA became fully operational in January 1990 with the appointment of its pioneer Chairman.

2.

NDLEA is Nigeria's lead institution in the war against illicit drug trafficking and abuse. The Agency is mandated to eliminate the growing, processing, manufacturing, selling, exporting, and trafficking of hard drugs and controlled substances throughout Nigeria and across its borders. The Agency is also empowered to identify, study, and treat drug-dependent individuals while creating public awareness on the dangers of drug abuse. NDLEA maintains a robust network of Zonal Commands spread across all states of the federation, with enforcement presence at all international airports, seaports, and major land border posts. The Agency collaborates with the United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime (UNODC), the International Narcotics Control Board (INCB), interpol, the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA), and other bilateral and multilateral partners in the global fight against narcotic drug trafficking.

3.

Since commencing operations in January 1990, NDLEA has recorded hundreds of tonnes of illicit drug seizures at Nigerian ports of entry and within the country's hinterland. The Agency has brought thousands of drug traffickers to justice and supported the conviction of major transnational drug syndicates. Under the current administration of Brig. Gen. MB Marwa (rtd) CON, OFR, which commenced in January 2021, NDLEA has achieved unprecedented operational successes, including record-breaking drug seizures that surpassed all previous annual records. The Agency has also significantly intensified its demand reduction campaign through programmes targeting schools, communities, religious bodies, and the entertainment industry. Through initiatives such as the Creatives Against Drug Abuse (CADA) and Operation LOUD (Let Our Youths Develop), NDLEA has deepened public engagement in the anti-drug campaign across all thirty-six states and the Federal Capital Territory.

4.

The National Drug Law Enforcement Agency operates under four core pillars that define its institutional character and guide its mandate. These pillars represent the Agency's holistic approach to winning the war against drug abuse — recognising that effective anti-narcotics work requires not only enforcement but also prevention, treatment, and global partnership:

Drug Law Enforcement

Intercepting and seizing illicit drugs at borders, airports, seaports, and within Nigeria — bringing traffickers to justice and dismantling drug supply networks.

Prevention & Awareness

Educating communities, schools, and institutions on the dangers of drug abuse through sustained outreach campaigns, targeting especially the vulnerable youth population.

Treatment & Rehabilitation

Providing comprehensive care and rehabilitation support for drug-dependent individuals through NDLEA's network of counselling and treatment centres across the country.

International Cooperation

Collaborating with global anti-drug agencies, including UNODC, INCB, DEA, and INTERPOL, to tackle transnational drug trafficking networks and uphold treaty obligations.

5.

The National Drug Law Enforcement Agency remains Nigeria's foremost anti-narcotics institution, ceaselessly working to protect the Nigerian citizenry from the devastating effects of illicit drug trafficking and abuse. Over three decades since its establishment, NDLEA has evolved into a modern, intelligence-driven law enforcement institution with national and international reach. Its dedicated officers continue to demonstrate courage, professionalism, and sacrifice in the service of the nation. The Agency's motto — "Ridding Society of Drug Abuse" — encapsulates its enduring commitment to a safer, healthier, and drug-free Nigeria.
